# About Edoxen

**Edoxen** is a set of information models used for representing
resolution and decision information. They provide a structured way to
model formal resolutions — including their metadata, considerations,
actions, and approvals — across many different standards organizations
and governance bodies.

## The name

"Edoxen" is how all resolutions of Ancient Athens started:

> *"It was the opinion of... (the people and city that...)"*

The word originates from the Ancient Greek **edokeō** (ἔδοξεν), meaning
"it was the opinion of" or "it seemed good to". This term was used in
the context of formal resolutions and decisions made by the Athenian
assembly, reflecting the collective will and judgment of the citizens.

## Applicability

The Edoxen information model covers the resolution structures of:

- ISO, ISO/TMB
- ITU-T, ITU-D, WTSA
- BIPM / CGPM
- OIML
- ILO
